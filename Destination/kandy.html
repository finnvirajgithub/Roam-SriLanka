Kandy

Home Destinations Kandy

"The Hill Capital"

Kandy, known as "The Hill Capital" of Sri Lanka, is a vibrant city surrounded by lush hills and tea plantations. It is renowned for the Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic, which houses a revered relic of Buddha and attracts pilgrims and visitors alike. As the last stronghold of the Sinhalese kings, Kandy played a crucial role in preserving Sri Lankan culture and traditions. The city’s rich history, colonial architecture, and scenic beauty make it a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a must-visit destination for cultural enthusiasts and nature lovers.

History

Kandy was the last capital of the ancient kings of Sri Lanka before the island fell under British rule in 1815. It played a vital role in preserving Sinhalese culture and Buddhism during a time of colonial expansion. The city’s most famous landmark, the Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic, is believed to house a tooth of the Buddha, making it a major pilgrimage site for Buddhists worldwide.

The city is also known for its role in resisting foreign domination, particularly during the Portuguese and Dutch colonial periods. Despite being the last kingdom to fall to European powers, Kandy's influence on Sri Lankan culture remains strong today, especially through its preservation of Buddhist traditions and architecture.

Key Attractions

  • Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic: This temple is the most important religious site in Kandy, housing a relic of the Buddha. Pilgrims from all over the world visit to pay their respects, and the temple complex itself is a masterpiece of traditional Kandyan architecture.
  • Kandy Lake: Located in the center of the city, this man-made lake provides a peaceful retreat amidst the bustling streets of Kandy. A stroll around the lake offers scenic views and a glimpse of the daily life in the city.
  • Peradeniya Royal Botanical Gardens: These sprawling gardens are located just outside Kandy and are home to a vast collection of tropical plants, orchids, and ancient trees. It’s a popular spot for nature lovers and those seeking relaxation.
  • Bahirawakanda Vihara Buddha Statue: A massive Buddha statue perched on a hill overlooking Kandy, offering panoramic views of the city and surrounding landscape. It's a peaceful place for contemplation and photography.
  • Kandy Esala Perahera: One of the grandest festivals in Sri Lanka, the Kandy Esala Perahera is a spectacular procession held annually to honor the Sacred Tooth Relic. It features traditional dancers, drummers, fire performances, and beautifully adorned elephants.
  • Kandy Viewpoint: For breathtaking views of the city, visitors can hike or drive up to this scenic viewpoint, offering a panoramic look at Kandy's natural beauty.

Cultural and Historical Significance

Kandy holds immense cultural and religious importance for Sri Lanka, particularly due to the presence of the Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic. The city is a center for traditional Sri Lankan art, dance, and music, especially evident during the annual Kandy Esala Perahera festival.

With its rich history and religious significance, Kandy remains a living symbol of Sri Lankan identity and spirituality. The city's lush landscapes, historical landmarks, and cultural festivals continue to attract visitors from around the world, offering an immersive experience in the country’s Buddhist heritage and natural beauty.
